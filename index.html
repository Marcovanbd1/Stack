The Neighbourhood
Health Stack

Five layers of neighbourhood health infrastructure.

Each one unlocks the next.

The sequence and the dependencies are the point.

What this is

A connected sequence of infrastructure, from the space before demand to the support that follows the appointment.

The Neighbourhood Health Stack is not a single product or platform. It is five connected layers of health infrastructure, built to cover the full journey a person takes through the neighbourhood health system. The layers run in sequence. Each one depends on the one before it. Together they cover the ground that no single tool, triage system, or patient platform reaches on its own.

The foundation layer is already live in Weymouth and Portland. It is free to replicate. The civic listening pilot is complete. Two layers require a procurement decision. The decisions made at Layers 1 and 2 shape everything that becomes possible at Layer 4. The sequence is the point. A layer deployed in one practice is a tool. Deployed across the neighbourhood, it is infrastructure.

The five layers

The stack moves a person from the space before demand, through the clinical encounter, to navigated support on the other side. Remove a layer or reorder the sequence, and the layers above it stop working.

L0
Navigation Intelligence
Live, accessible, neighbourhood-wide information about what exists and where to go. Built for utility, not completeness. Nothing is included because it ought to be there. Everything is included because it is genuinely useful to a real person trying to find something real. This is the layer that addresses the space before demand. It is live in Weymouth and Portland. It is free to replicate. It is also the infrastructure on which Layer 3 is built.
Live
L1
Smart Triage
Equitable, intelligent routing to the right care, first time. Access to the right pathway should not depend on how articulate, persistent, or digitally confident a patient is. Neighbourhood-wide adoption is what turns a triage tool into an access equity intervention. Eighteen months of clinical observation precede this procurement decision. The supplier becomes part of the neighbourhood health infrastructure for the long term. Clinical safety discipline and NHS alignment matter more here than innovation credentials.
Demo stage Open procurement
L2
Ambient Voice Technology
The clinical encounter becomes fully present. The clinician is with the patient, not the screen. Every credible platform has solved transcription accuracy. The question is what happens to the structured output after the consultation ends: how it reaches the patient, the care team, and the record. The supplier chosen at Layer 2 determines what becomes possible at Layer 4. That relationship should be chosen with that consequence in mind.
Demo stage Lexacom
L3
Neighbourhood Signals
Built on top of Layer 0, the navigation infrastructure becomes a democratic instrument. The pages people loop between, the dead ends, the drop-offs, the QR scans that cluster in specific streets: these are not analytics. They are the neighbourhood telling you where pressure is forming before it becomes demand on services. The 91-day pilot in Weymouth and Portland is complete. Three signal clusters identified. Zero personal data collected.
Pilot complete
L4
Support Playlists
A calm, bespoke, sequenced set of next steps. Available before an appointment, during it, or after it ends. Platform agnostic. Free to patients and residents at the point of use, with no market surface in primary care. The structured output from Layer 2 makes personalised playlists possible at scale. This is what the whole stack has been building toward. It is only possible because of what Layer 2 puts in place.
