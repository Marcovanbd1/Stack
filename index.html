The procurement map

Three layers involve a supplier. The rest is built, owned, and free to replicate. The ideal is one supplier across all three — and the market is moving that way.

01
Layer 1 · Access
AI Smart Triage
A Class IIb medical device. DCB0160 compliant. Twelve months of clinical observation precede deployment. The procurement category is well-established. The clinical safety discipline is not always applied — it must be.
02
Layer 2 · Encounter
Ambient Voice Technology
A Class IIa medical device. The frontier is not transcription accuracy — it is what happens to the structured output after the consultation ends. The supplier relationship at Layer 2 determines everything at Layer 4.
03
Layers 2 and 4 · Encounter and Action
Workflow Solutions
The encounter generates structured output. What happens to that output — how it reaches the patient, the team, the record — is a workflow question. A good AVT platform should include this as standard. The neighbourhoods that procure AVT without workflow will build the gap back in.

"Beyond the foundational interventions, we will not prescribe an approach. Local government and ICBs know their local communities best."

The Stack is what local knowledge produces when it is given the space to build from practice rather than from compliance. It was built before the Framework was published. The Framework described it accurately. That is not a coincidence — it is what good policy does when it catches up with good practice.
